01 / 10 — ON-PAGE SEO

The elements Google reads
on every single page

On-page SEO = what you control directly. Title, headings, content, images, URLs. This is where most people start — and where most people make mistakes.

The Title Tag — Most Important On-Page Factor

The <title> tag appears in three places: browser tab, Google search results, and social shares. It's the biggest signal Google uses to understand page topic.

❌ Bad Title
Home | My Website

No keyword. Not descriptive. Wastes the most valuable SEO real estate.

❌ Too Long (80 chars)
The Best Comprehensive Guide to Understanding Search Engine Optimization for Beginners in 2024

Gets truncated in SERPs. Google rewrites it. Power lost.

✅ Great Title (~58 chars)
SEO Guide for Beginners: Rank #1 on Google (2024)

Primary keyword first. Benefit clear. Year signals freshness. Under 60 chars.

✅ Product Page Title
iPhone 15 Pro Case – Slim & Drop-Proof | CaseCo

Keyword + USP + brand. Dash separates brand. Conversational.

RuleWhy It Matters
50–60 characters maxGoogle truncates longer titles in SERPs with "..."

Every page uniqueDuplicate titles = Google can't differentiate pages
Match search intent"how to" title for tutorial, not "buy" title
Include brand for homepageBrand recognition at end: "Topic | Brand Name"

URL Structure — Clean, Descriptive, Keyword-Rich

❌ Bad URLs
example.com/?p=4921
example.com/page1.html
example.com/SEO-GUIDE-2024-best-tips-beginner
✅ Good URLs
example.com/seo-guide
example.com/blog/dog-training-tips
example.com/tools/keyword-research
  • ✅ Lowercase letters only — uppercase can cause duplicate URL issues
  • ✅ Hyphens (-) not underscores (_) — Google treats underscores as word joiners
  • ✅ Include target keyword — "seo-guide" not "article-5"
  • ✅ Short as possible — remove stop words (and, the, a, of)
  • ✅ Use subfolders for structure — /blog/topic not /blog-topic
  • ❌ Never change URLs on existing pages — destroys link equity

Image SEO — Google Can't See Images (Yet)

Google reads text around images and the alt attribute to understand what an image contains. Images also appear in Google Image Search — a traffic source most sites ignore.

<!-- ❌ Bad image --> <img src="IMG_00432.jpg" /> <!-- ✅ Good image --> <img src="golden-retriever-training-sit-command.webp" alt="Golden retriever sitting on command during training session" width="800" height="600" loading="lazy" />
01

File Name

Descriptive, hyphenated. "dog-training.jpg" not "image1.jpg"

02

Alt Text

Describe the image in plain English. Include keyword naturally. Don't keyword stuff.

03

File Format

Use WebP (best). AVIF (better). JPEG for photos. PNG for graphics. Never BMP/TIFF.

04

File Size

Compress before upload. Target under 150KB. Use Squoosh or Sharp.

05

Dimensions

Specify width/height to prevent layout shift (CLS). Never skip these.

06

Lazy Loading

Add loading="lazy" to off-screen images. Speeds up initial load.

02 / 10 — TECHNICAL SEO

What Google sees before
it reads a single word

Technical SEO ensures Google can find, crawl, understand, and index your site. Without this foundation, even great content won't rank.

The Crawl → Index → Rank Pipeline

STEP 1
Crawling

Googlebot follows links across the web, downloading page HTML. If it can't access a page (blocked, broken, too slow), it can't rank it.

STEP 2
Processing

Google renders the page (executes JavaScript), extracts content, identifies links. It now understands what the page says.

STEP 3
Indexing

Processed pages enter Google's index — a database of 100+ billion webpages. Only indexed pages can rank. Check via "site:yourdomain.com".

STEP 4
Ranking

For each query, Google ranks indexed pages by 200+ factors. Your technical setup, content, and links determine position.

robots.txt — The Bouncer for Googlebot

Located at yourdomain.com/robots.txt. Tells crawlers which pages NOT to crawl. Common mistake: accidentally blocking your own site.

# Allow everything (default for most sites) User-agent: * Allow: / # Block crawling of admin, thank-you pages User-agent: * Disallow: /wp-admin/ Disallow: /thank-you/ Disallow: /cart/ # Point to sitemap Sitemap: https://example.com/sitemap.xml
⚠️ Critical Mistake: Robots.txt blocks CRAWLING, not INDEXING. If other sites link to a blocked page, Google may still index it without seeing the content. Use noindex meta tag to prevent indexing.

XML Sitemap — Your Site's Table of Contents for Google

Lists all important URLs on your site with metadata. Submit to Google Search Console. Especially vital for large sites or new sites with few inbound links.

<?xml version="1.0" encoding="UTF-8"?> <urlset xmlns="http://www.sitemaps.org/schemas/sitemap/0.9"> <url> <loc>https://example.com/</loc> <lastmod>2024-03-15</lastmod> <changefreq>weekly</changefreq> <priority>1.0</priority> </url> <url> <loc>https://example.com/blog/dog-training</loc> <lastmod>2024-03-10</lastmod> <priority>0.8</priority> </url> </urlset>

HTTPS (SSL Certificate)

Google confirmed HTTPS as a ranking signal in 2014. Chrome marks HTTP sites as "Not Secure." Every site must use HTTPS. Get a free SSL cert from Let's Encrypt or your host.

Also: ensure HTTP redirects to HTTPS. www redirects to non-www (or vice versa). One canonical version of every URL.

Mobile-First Indexing

Google uses the mobile version of your site for indexing and ranking (since 2019). If your mobile site is missing content that the desktop has, you won't rank for it.

  • ✅ Responsive design
  • ✅ Same content on mobile/desktop
  • ✅ Touch-friendly tap targets (48px min)
  • ✅ No horizontal scrolling

03 / 10 — KEYWORD RESEARCH

Finding what people actually
type into Google

Keywords aren't just words — they represent intent. Understanding why someone searches is more important than what they search.

The Keyword Landscape

puppy training dog training tips how to train a dog positive reinforcement dog training puppy obedience training dog behavior clicker training treat-based training how to train a puppy not to bite puppy training schedule 8 weeks how long does puppy training take
■ Primary (head) keyword — High volume, high competition
■ Secondary keyword — Medium volume, more specific
■ LSI / semantic terms — Related concepts Google expects to see
■ Long-tail — Low volume but HIGH intent, easy to rank

Search Intent — Why Google's #1 Priority is Intent

Google's entire goal is to match the searcher's intent. If you write a blog post for a keyword where people want to buy something, you won't rank — even with perfect SEO. Intent must match content type.

Intent TypeExample QueryWhat User WantsBest Content Type
Informational "how to train a puppy" Learn something Blog post, guide, video
Navigational "Petco puppy classes" Find specific site/page Brand's homepage or specific page
Commercial "best puppy training books" Research before buying Comparison, review, listicle
Transactional "buy dog training collar" Purchase now Product page, category page
Local "puppy training classes near me" Find local service Local landing page + Google Business Profile
Test: Google your target keyword. Look at the top 10 results. What type of content is ranking? That tells you what Google considers the right intent match. Create that type of content.

What Makes a Good Keyword Target?

Volume

Monthly Search Volume

How many people search this per month. Higher = more potential traffic. But high volume = high competition. New sites should target 100–1,000 volume keywords.

Tool: Ahrefs, Semrush, Google Keyword Planner
KD

Keyword Difficulty

0–100 score. How hard to rank #1. Determined mainly by backlinks to top-ranking pages. New sites: target KD under 20. Established sites: up to 60.

Score from 0 (easy) to 100 (impossible)
CPC

Cost Per Click

What advertisers pay per click in Google Ads. High CPC = high commercial value. Great proxy for keywords worth ranking for organically.

"Insurance" CPC = $60+ per click
CTR

Click-Through Rate

Position 1 gets ~28% of clicks. Position 10 gets ~2%. Featured snippets can steal clicks without users visiting. Factor this into priority.

Average CTR drops 90% from pos 1 to pos 10

The Keyword Research Process (Step by Step)

  1. Seed keywords — brainstorm 5–10 broad topics your site covers
  2. Expand — use Ahrefs/Semrush to find related keywords, questions, and autocomplete variations
  3. Analyze intent — Google each keyword. What's ranking? Match that format.
  4. Filter by volume + difficulty — sweet spot is decent volume + low difficulty
  5. Group by topic — cluster related keywords. One page targets one keyword cluster, not 50 separate keywords.
  6. Map to pages — assign keyword clusters to existing or new pages. Never target same keyword on two pages (keyword cannibalization).
  7. Prioritize — quick wins first (low difficulty, decent volume)

Anchor Text — The Words Used to Link

The clickable text of a link tells Google what the destination page is about. A link saying "puppy training guide" to your page tells Google your page is about puppy training.

Penguin Penalty: If 80%+ of backlinks use exact-match anchor text, Google suspects manipulation and may penalize. Natural link profiles are diverse.

Internal Links — Often the Most Overlooked SEO Lever

Internal links distribute "link equity" across your site. They also tell Google which pages are most important (more internal links = more important). Use them strategically.

Pillar Page Model:
1 main "pillar" page on broad topic → links to many specific "cluster" pages → cluster pages link back to pillar. This signals topical authority.
  • ✅ Link from high-authority pages to new pages
  • ✅ Use descriptive, keyword-rich anchor text
  • ✅ Every page should have at least 2–3 internal links pointing to it
  • ❌ Don't use nofollow on internal links
  • ❌ Don't link the same anchor to two different pages

How to Actually Get Backlinks

Digital PR

Create data-driven studies, surveys, or original research. Journalists link to original data. One study can generate hundreds of links.

Difficulty: Hard | Quality: Very High

Guest Posting

Write articles for other sites in your niche. Include a link back. Google accepts this if done in moderation with genuine value.

Difficulty: Medium | Quality: Medium

Broken Link Building

Find broken links on other sites. Offer your content as replacement. Win-win: they fix a broken link, you get a backlink.

Difficulty: Medium | Quality: High

Skyscraper Technique

Find content with many backlinks. Create a better version. Ask people linking to original to link to yours instead.

Difficulty: Hard | Quality: High

Resource Pages

Find pages that list resources in your niche. Ask to be added. "dog training resources" + site:edu often works well.

Difficulty: Easy | Quality: Medium

HARO / Qwoted

Journalists ask for expert sources. Respond quickly with genuine expertise. Can earn links from major publications.

Difficulty: Medium | Quality: Very High
05 / 10 — CONTENT STRATEGY

E-E-A-T: Google's quality
framework for ranking

Google wants to rank content from people with real experience, expertise, authority, and trustworthiness. This is E-E-A-T — the framework behind every quality assessment.

Experience · Expertise · Authoritativeness · Trustworthiness

🧪
Experience

Did the author actually DO the thing? A review of hiking boots written by someone who hiked 100 miles in them > review by someone who never wore them.

🎓
Expertise

Does the author have credentials, skills, or knowledge? Medical content by doctors, legal content by lawyers. Display credentials clearly.

🏛️
Authoritativeness

Is the author and site recognized as an authority? Backlinks from credible sites, mentions in media, citations by other experts — all build this.

🔒
Trustworthiness

Most important factor. HTTPS, clear contact info, privacy policy, accurate content, no hidden ads. Trust is foundational.

YMYL (Your Money or Your Life): Health, finance, legal, safety topics get extra scrutiny. Google holds these to the highest E-E-A-T standards because bad information can directly harm users. If your site is YMYL, E-E-A-T isn't optional.

Content Depth — Not Length, but Coverage

Word count is not a ranking factor. Depth of coverage is. A 500-word article that perfectly answers a query beats a 5,000-word one that rambles. But complex topics naturally require more words.

Content TypeTypical LengthPriority
Blog post / Guide1,500–3,000 wordsCover all subtopics. Include images, examples.
Product page300–800 wordsSpecs, benefits, reviews. Clear CTA.
Homepage300–500 wordsClear value proposition. Internal links to key pages.
Category page200–400 words intro + productsUseful intro + subcategory links + products
Pillar page3,000–8,000 wordsComprehensive. Links to all cluster pages.
FAQ page1,000–2,000 wordsTarget PAA (People Also Ask) questions

Freshness — Why Updating Content Beats Creating New Content

Google's QDF (Query Deserves Freshness) algorithm boosts fresh content for time-sensitive topics. But even evergreen content benefits from regular updates — it shows Google the page is maintained.

  • ✅ Update publish date when content is substantially updated (not just for show)
  • ✅ Add new sections when topic evolves. Don't delete old content unless it's wrong.
  • ✅ Use lastmod in sitemap to tell Google when pages were updated
  • ✅ Regular audits: find pages losing traffic and refresh them
  • ❌ Don't change the date without updating content — Google detects this

07 / 10 — LOCAL SEO

Ranking in your city,
your neighborhood, your street

For businesses serving a local area, local SEO is often more valuable than regular SEO. "Near me" searches convert at extremely high rates.

The 3 Local Ranking Factors

📏 Proximity

Physical distance between searcher and business. You can't control this — but you can ensure your address is accurate everywhere.

⭐ Prominence

How well-known the business is. Reviews, backlinks from local sites, mentions, citations in directories all build prominence.

🎯 Relevance

Does your Google Business Profile and website match what the user is searching for? Correct categories, services, and keywords.

Google Business Profile (GBP)

Free tool. Controls your "Map Pack" listing. Most important local SEO asset. Claim it at business.google.com.

  • ✅ Complete all fields (100% profile)
  • ✅ Choose correct primary category
  • ✅ Add all services + descriptions
  • ✅ Upload real photos (businesses with photos get 35% more clicks)
  • ✅ Post weekly updates (like social media)
  • ✅ Respond to every review (positive AND negative)
  • ✅ Add Q&A section answers

NAP: Name, Address, Phone

Your business name, address, and phone must be IDENTICAL everywhere on the web. Inconsistencies confuse Google and hurt local rankings.

  • 📍 Website footer
  • 📍 Google Business Profile
  • 📍 Yelp, TripAdvisor, Yellow Pages
  • 📍 Apple Maps, Bing Places
  • 📍 Industry-specific directories
  • 📍 Social media profiles
"Suite 100" vs "Ste 100" vs "Ste. 100" are different to Google's parser. Pick one format.
08 / 10 — CORE WEB VITALS

Page experience: Google
now ranks how pages feel

Since 2021, Google uses Core Web Vitals as a ranking signal. Speed isn't just good UX — it's a ranking factor. These are the three metrics you must optimize.

LCP
Largest Contentful Paint
Time to render the largest visible element. Target: under 2.5 seconds. Usually: hero image or H1.
GOOD: < 2.5s
NEEDS WORK: 2.5–4s
POOR: > 4s
INP
Interaction to Next Paint
How fast page responds to clicks/taps. Replaced FID in 2024. Target: under 200ms. Usually: JavaScript blocking.
GOOD: < 200ms
NEEDS WORK: 200–500ms
POOR: > 500ms
CLS
Cumulative Layout Shift
Visual stability. Elements jumping around as page loads. Target: under 0.1. Usually: images without dimensions, late-loading ads.
GOOD: < 0.1
NEEDS WORK: 0.1–0.25
POOR: > 0.25
TTFB
Time to First Byte
How fast server responds. Not a CWV but affects LCP. Target: under 800ms. Fix: better hosting, CDN, caching.
GOOD: < 800ms

Practical Fixes for Each Metric

ProblemMetric AffectedFix
Slow hero imageLCPCompress to WebP, preload hero image: <link rel="preload" as="image">
Render-blocking JSLCP, INPAdd defer/async to scripts. Move non-critical JS to bottom.
No image dimensionsCLSAlways set width + height attributes on every <img>
Late-loading fontsCLS, LCPPreconnect to font CDN. Use font-display: swap.
Large JavaScript bundleINPCode split. Remove unused JS. Lazy load below-fold components.
Slow hostingTTFB, LCPUse CDN (Cloudflare). Upgrade hosting. Enable caching.
Unoptimized imagesLCPUse srcset. Serve correct size per device. WebP format.
Measure: Use PageSpeed Insights (pagespeed.web.dev) + Chrome DevTools Lighthouse + Google Search Console → Core Web Vitals report
09 / 10 — SERP FEATURES

Owning more real estate
on the results page

Modern Google SERPs are full of features beyond the 10 blue links. Each feature has specific triggers. Understanding them lets you claim multiple positions at once.

Position 0

Featured Snippet

The answer box above all results. Triggered by question queries. Format your answer in: paragraph (what is X), numbered list (how to), or table (comparison). Put the direct answer in a <p> or <ul> immediately after the H2 question.

Trigger: "how to", "what is", "vs", definition queries
PAA

People Also Ask

Expandable question boxes. Infinite scroll of questions. Answer PAA questions in your content with exact question as H3, concise 40–60 word answer below, then detailed explanation. FAQ Schema accelerates this.

Trigger: questions related to main query
Map Pack

Local Pack (3 Results)

The map + 3 local business listings. Appears for local-intent queries. Driven by Google Business Profile + reviews + proximity. Separate from regular organic rankings.

Trigger: "near me", city + service queries
Image Pack

Image Results

Row of images inline in results. Win by: descriptive alt text, image filename, surrounding text context, fast load, correct image schema. Great for visual products and tutorials.

Trigger: visual topics, products, food, travel
Sitelinks

Navigation Sitelinks

Sub-links beneath your brand result. Automatically generated by Google based on your site structure and anchor text of internal links. Can't force them — build clear navigation instead.

Trigger: branded queries
KP

Knowledge Panel

Information box on the right side for brands, people, organizations. Built from Wikipedia, Wikidata, and your own structured data. Claim via Google Search Console for verified entities.

Trigger: brand/entity searches

Debunked: What Doesn't Affect Rankings

MythReality
Meta keywords tagGoogle ignored it since 2009. Still shows up in old guides. Skip it.
Keyword density (% of page)There's no ideal density. Write naturally. Don't count occurrences.
Submitting to GoogleGooglebot finds your site via links. No need to submit unless using GSC sitemap.
More pages = betterQuality > quantity. Thin pages hurt rankings. Content pruning often helps.
Google Ads boost organicPaid and organic are completely separate. Running ads has zero SEO effect.
Social media rankingsSocial signals are not a confirmed ranking factor. Indirect effects only (traffic, links).
